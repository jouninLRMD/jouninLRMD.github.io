Featured Research Projects

My strength lies in creativity, enabling me to devise novel solutions across various topics! Four selected featured research achievements:

2022 - 2023

Pioneering Graph Signal Processing for Biomedical Signals

Developed the first Graph Signal Processing method to analyze biomedical signals, introducing a novel paradigm for understanding physiological data. This approach transforms traditional time-series signals into graph structures, enabling the extraction of spatial and temporal patterns. Applied to EDA for emotion recognition, achieving state-of-the-art performance with interpretable features.

2023 - 2024

Graph-Based Detection of Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Created an innovative GSP framework for analyzing ERG signals to detect ASD and ADHD. This approach leverages graph-based representation of ERG waveforms to capture subtle abnormalities in retinal neural processing. The method demonstrates high classification accuracy while significantly reducing computational complexity, making it practical for clinical deployment.

2020 - 2025

Deep Learning for Multimodal Biomedical Signal Analysis

Developed advanced deep learning architectures for multimodal biomedical signal analysis, encompassing autoencoders for nonlinear feature extraction, CNNs for time-frequency analysis, and multimodal fusion techniques integrating EDA, ERG, ECG, and PPG. Applied to emotion recognition, pain intensity classification, and arrhythmia detection.

2024 - 2025

Graph Neural Networks for Stroke Detection & Segmentation

Engineered a sophisticated GNN framework with probabilistic attention mechanisms for detecting and segmenting stroke lesions from multimodal MRI data. It represents brain imaging data as graphs and achieves superior performance in stroke lesion detection while providing interpretable attention maps that align with clinical expertise.

The Journey Through My Expertise

My work lies at the intersection of life and computation. Rather than simply using tools, I weave them together to solve complex mysteries hidden within the human body and mind. Here is the story of the technologies that power my research.

Architecting the Mind: Advanced AI

To unravel the complexities of human physiology, I rely on the robust foundations of PyTorch and PyTorch Geometric. My passion lies in designing Probabilistic Graph Attention Networks and applying Bayesian Methods to navigate the uncertainty inherent in medical data. By blending these advanced deep learning architectures with emerging generative models, I build AI systems capable of perceiving the subtle, interconnected patterns of life.

Decoding the Body: Signals & Imaging

The human body speaks a language of high-dimensional signals, and I have dedicated my research to translating it. From the electrical whispers of the heart and brain (EKG, EEG) to the rhythmic flow of blood (PPG), I capture these physiological stories. I also venture deeper, utilizing MRI and fMRI to map the intricate structures and functions of the brain. Through Graph Neural Networks, I transform these vast modalities into coherent maps of human emotion and health.

The Languages of Creation

Every great architecture needs a solid foundation. My native tongue in the world of research is Python, the lingua franca of AI, and R for rigorous statistical inference. When performance and hardware-level control are paramount, particularly in processing massive biomedical datasets, I rely on the raw speed of C and C++. To build modern, safe, and highly concurrent systems, I am increasingly turning to the power of Rust and Go.

Featured Research

My research focuses on applying advanced machine learning and signal processing techniques to biomedical data. Here is a closer look at some of my recent work and the key findings driving my research forward.

Neurocomputing

Graph-Based Multi-Modal MRI Analysis

This research introduces a novel graph-based approach incorporating probabilistic attention mechanisms for detecting stroke lesions. By leveraging multi-modal MRI data, we significantly improve the accuracy and robustness of lesion segmentation, offering a powerful new tool for automated medical image analysis.

IEEE JBHI

EDA-Graph: Emotion Detection

We pioneered the use of Graph Signal Processing on Electrodermal Activity (EDA) to detect emotional states. By modeling physiological signals as a graph, this methodology captures complex non-linear dynamics often missed by traditional time-series analysis, achieving state-of-the-art results in emotion recognition.

Read Paper
MDPI Sensors

ERG-Graph: Neurodevelopmental Disorders

This study explores the application of graph-based analysis on Electroretinograms (ERG). By reducing computational complexity while retaining critical signal features, we demonstrated that ERG signals can be effectively used to classify neurodevelopmental disorders, presenting a rapid, non-invasive diagnostic pathway.
