Neurocomputing · 2025

Graph-Based Multi-Modal MRI with Probabilistic Attention for Stroke Lesion Detection

Fuses T1, T2, and FLAIR MRI into a single attention-weighted graph — sharper lesion maps, in a fraction of the compute.

Method

Represent each voxel neighborhood as a node and fuse three MRI modalities into a single graph. A probabilistic graph-attention network learns which connections carry lesion-relevant evidence — the uncertainty estimate doubles as an interpretability map for clinicians.

Why it matters

In stroke, minutes equal neurons. This approach delivers higher Dice scores than 3D-U-Net baselines while generating attention heatmaps that radiologists can sanity-check — closing the "black-box" gap that keeps deep learning out of acute care.

IEEE JBHI · 2024

EDA-Graph: Graph Signal Processing of Electrodermal Activity for Emotion Detection

The first graph-signal-processing pipeline for a biomedical signal — turns a 1-D sweat trace into a topological feature your classifier can actually see.

Method

Segment the EDA trace into nodes, weight edges by temporal & phasic similarity, then apply graph Fourier & wavelet transforms to derive spectral descriptors. The graph captures nonlinear sweat-gland dynamics that FFT-based baselines flatten out.

Why it matters

Emotion recognition in wearables lives or dies by the quality of a single skin-conductance channel. EDA-Graph pushed state-of-the-art on the continuous valence & arousal axes while staying cheap enough for on-device inference.

Bioengineering · 2026

ERG-Graph: Graph Signal Processing of the Electroretinogram for Neurodevelopmental Disorders

A 2-minute retinal recording, rendered as a graph, separates autism and ADHD from typical development with clinical-grade accuracy.

Method

Extract a-/b-wave peaks from ERG responses at two flash strengths, build a sparse similarity graph across waveform landmarks, and feed its graph-Fourier coefficients into a classical classifier. The graph discards redundant temporal samples while preserving the diagnostic topology.

Why it matters

Neurodevelopmental diagnosis today hinges on hours of behavioral assessment. An objective, non-invasive retinal biomarker — cheap enough to run in any vision clinic — could close diagnostic gaps for families who can't reach a specialist.
